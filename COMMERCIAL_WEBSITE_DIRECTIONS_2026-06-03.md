# AI PickGold Commercial Website Directions

Date: 2026-06-03
Domain: https://www.aipickgold.com
Scope: self-media operations, personal IP, knowledge creator, light entrepreneurship, AI workflow tools

## Executive Conclusion

The highest-value website is not a generic personal homepage, not a pure chatbot, and not only a Markdown formatter.

The strongest commercial direction is:

> AI PickGold Studio: a Skill-first creator operations workbench that turns one source idea into WeChat articles, Xiaohongshu cards, X/Twitter threads, short-video scripts, publish packages, and API/Skill outputs, while keeping final account publishing under human control.

This fits the user's existing assets unusually well:

- WxMD already proves the Markdown-to-WeChat formatter.
- CardMD already proves the Xiaohongshu card direction.
- The local self-media system already has source-unit templates, review gates, XHS/X/WeChat/Bilibili platform rules, and manual publish handoff.
- HyperFrames already points toward video-as-code education and course/product packaging.
- Codex/GitHub/Product Design/Linear/Figma can become part of the visible product story: "I build and operate the same workflow I teach."

The commercial bet should be:

1. Make the current site an actual creator workbench.
2. Add a Skill/API Center around WxMD, CardMD, publish packages, and video packages.
3. Use personal IP content to prove the workflow weekly.
4. Sell subscriptions, API credits, template packs, course kits, and custom workflow setup.

## Market Signals

These signals support a workflow/product-first site instead of a content-only blog.

1. Creator spend is moving from simple influencer placement to creator-led systems.
   - IAB's 2025 creator economy ad spend strategy report frames creator investment as a serious media/business channel rather than a niche experiment.
   - Source: https://www.iab.com/insights/2025-creator-economy-ad-spend-strategy-report/

2. Creator tooling is becoming an operations layer.
   - Kit's recent Email MCP launch is a good example of creator tools connecting directly into AI assistants and workflows, not just providing a web UI.
   - Source: https://kit.com/news/kit-launches-email-mcp

3. AI video is commercially hot, but the market is crowded.
   - Grand View Research tracks AI video generator as a fast-growing category. This supports the video workflow opportunity, but also means a generic "AI video generator" would be hard to defend.
   - Source: https://www.grandviewresearch.com/industry-analysis/ai-video-generator-market-report

4. Chatbots and agents are growing, but standalone bots are commoditized.
   - Chatbot market growth supports adding an assistant, but not making "chat with my bot" the whole product. The bot needs proprietary context: cases, templates, source units, publishing rules, and workflow memory.
   - Source: https://www.grandviewresearch.com/industry-analysis/chatbot-market

5. Chinese AI content operations need explicit compliance boundaries.
   - The CAC AI-generated/synthetic content labeling rules make platform-safe disclosure and review gates part of the product value.
   - Source: https://www.cac.gov.cn/2025-03/14/c_1743654684782215.htm

6. Platform automation boundaries matter.
   - X has explicit automation rules.
   - WeChat has official draft/publish APIs, but public-account operations still require careful token and preview handling.
   - Bilibili has an official open platform, but real upload workflows still need platform-specific packaging.
   - Xiaohongshu commercialization happens through official creator/business systems such as 蒲公英, while publishing should remain manual unless a compliant official path is confirmed.
   - Sources:
     - https://help.x.com/en/rules-and-policies/x-automation
     - https://docs.x.com/x-api/overview
     - https://developers.weixin.qq.com/doc/offiaccount/Draft_Box/Add_draft.html
     - https://developers.weixin.qq.com/doc/offiaccount/Publish/Publish.html
     - https://openhome.bilibili.com/doc
     - https://pgy.xiaohongshu.com/

## Direction 1: AI PickGold Studio Workbench

### Website Shape

The first screen should be the product itself:

- Left: source Markdown / source unit.
- Middle: output switcher.
- Right: live preview or publish-package status.
- Top switcher: WeChat, Xiaohongshu, X, Video, API.
- Bottom status: needs review, ready for manual publish, blocked, exported.

Suggested pages:

- `/studio`
- `/studio/packages`
- `/studio/history`
- `/pricing`
- `/docs/publish-package`

### What It Sells

Customers are not buying "themes"; they are buying the last-mile conversion from good draft to publishable package.

Commercial promises:

- Turn one Markdown draft into platform-ready assets.
- Keep a consistent visual/content style.
- Avoid rewriting the same idea from scratch for each platform.
- Preserve compliance and manual publish gates.
- Export packages that can be reviewed by a human.

### MVP Features

- Source-unit editor.
- WxMD output.
- CardMD output.
- X thread outline output.
- Short-video script output.
- Publish package manifest.
- Manual publish checklist.
- Export folder or downloadable zip.
- Visible AI disclosure/risk checklist.

### Monetization

- Free: limited themes, limited exports, watermark.
- Pro: unlimited WxMD/CardMD exports.
- Studio: publish package builder, history, saved templates, API key.
- Custom setup: creator workflow migration and theme customization.

Suggested early pricing test:

- Pro monthly: RMB 29-49.
- Studio monthly: RMB 99-199.
- Lifetime early founder plan: RMB 399-899.
- Custom setup: RMB 999-2999 for individual creators; RMB 4999+ for small teams.

### Why This Is Best

It converts existing work into a product quickly, while matching the personal IP: "I publicly operate the workflow I sell."

## Direction 2: Skill/API Center

### Website Shape

This should be the commercial developer/AI-operator surface.

Suggested pages:

- `/skills`
- `/skills/wxmd`
- `/skills/cardmd`
- `/api`
- `/api/playground`
- `/docs/codex-skill`
- `/docs/claude-skill`

### Productized APIs

Start with:

- `POST /api/convert`: Markdown to WeChat HTML.
- Card export API: Markdown or structured card JSON to Xiaohongshu card set.
- Publish-package API: source unit to multi-platform manifest.
- Theme API: list themes, preview theme, validate style.

Do not start with direct platform posting as the default selling point.

### Skill Products

Create installable prompts/skills:

- WxMD Skill: take a Markdown essay and return WeChat-ready HTML.
- CardMD Skill: turn a tutorial into 5-7 Xiaohongshu cards.
- Creator Package Skill: turn one source unit into X, XHS, WeChat, and video-script variants.
- Review Skill: platform risk, AI label, CTA, title, and overclaim check.
- HyperFrames Video Skill: turn a case into a short-video storyboard and render spec.

### Monetization

- API credit packs.
- Monthly API quota.
- Team license.
- Custom Skill build service.
- Private deployment for teams with sensitive content.

### Commercial Advantage

This is the clearest bridge from Codex/GitHub/Product Design usage to a real product. It also makes the site useful to technical creators, agencies, and operators who do not want only a web editor.

## Direction 5: Personal IP Knowledge Bot

### Website Shape

Suggested pages:

- `/ask`
- `/ask/workflow`
- `/ask/templates`

### What The Bot Should Do

The bot should not be a generic "chat with me" widget. It should answer from public, curated workflow material:

- How to turn one idea into XHS cards.
- How to convert a WeChat article from Markdown.
- Which platform package fits a topic.
- Which risk checklist applies.
- Which template to use.
- How to price a creator workflow product.

### Data Source

Use public docs and cases only at first:

- Site docs.
- Case library.
- Template explanations.
- Public personal-IP playbooks.
- Public changelog.

Avoid feeding private notes, tokens, unpublished finances, private client data, or private account details into a public bot.

### Monetization

- Free limited Q&A.
- Pro users get "generate package from this answer."
- Studio users get saved workflows and custom brand context.
- Custom bot build service for creators/teams.

### Recommendation

Build this after at least 10-20 public cases exist. A bot without a strong case library will feel like a wrapper. A bot on top of real cases becomes a sales assistant.

## Direction 7: Public Equity / Tushare Research Site

This is valuable but should not be mixed into the initial AI PickGold creator site.

Reasons:

- Different buyer.
- Higher compliance risk.
- Personal IP currently promises "opportunity research with risk conditions," not investment advice.
- It can distract from the clearer creator-tool business.

Recommendation:

- Keep public equity research as a separate Obsidian/local artifact loop.
- If productized later, create a separate sub-brand focused on research workflow, not recommendations.
- Never combine "creator monetization tool" and "market opportunity" in a way that looks like return promises.

## Do Not Build First

Avoid these early traps:

1. One-click cross-platform auto-publishing bot.
   - High platform/compliance risk.
   - Weak trust.
   - Bad fit with current manual publish safety boundary.

2. Pure chatbot homepage.
   - Too generic.
   - Weak monetization unless backed by cases/templates/workflows.

3. Generic prompt marketplace.
   - Low moat.
   - Hard to differentiate.

4. Pure AI video generator wrapper.
   - Crowded market.
   - Expensive.
   - Hard to defend without workflow proof.

5. Mixing public equity advice into creator-tool homepage.
   - Audience mismatch.
   - Compliance and trust risk.
